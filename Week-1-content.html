Week 1 HCI Notes

Week 1 New Terms

HCI: Human-Computer Interaction studies how humans interact with computers and systems, focusing on usability, accessibility, and efficiency.

Cognitive Science: Explores the mind, including memory, perception, reasoning, and problem-solving.

Interaction: Actions and communication between humans and computer systems.

STM (Short-Term Memory): Limited capacity memory; Miller’s Law suggests humans can hold 7 ± 2 items at once.

LTM (Long-Term Memory): Stores knowledge and experiences permanently, often organized in semantic networks.

Chunking: Grouping information into meaningful units to reduce cognitive load and improve memorability.

Semantic Network: A representation of knowledge where concepts are connected by categories, properties, and examples.

Interface Chunking Observations

Chunking organizes content into meaningful groups so users do not have to process everything at once, reducing cognitive load.

YouTube Example

The YouTube side panel groups content into clear sections: account and search at the top, navigation sections like Home, Subscriptions, and Explore in separate boxes, and personal items like History and Playlists together. Users can focus on one category at a time without feeling overwhelmed.

Canva Example

The Canva dashboard separates features into labeled sections: "Explore Templates" for template types, editing tools in another, and updates in their own section. This grouping allows users to focus on one type of task at a time.

Short-Term Memory (STM) & Miller’s Law

Short-term memory has limited capacity. Miller’s Law suggests that most people can hold 7 ± 2 items in STM. Interfaces should respect this limit to reduce cognitive overload.

DataCamp Example

The DataCamp interface separates content into sections: top banner offers deals, left sidebar navigation is grouped into Progress and LEARN, tracks and courses are grouped, and the main course listing is displayed in cards. This respects STM limitations and allows users to focus without overload.

Etsy Example

Etsy groups homepage content into labeled sections. The search bar is separate, main navigation contains only 5 tabs, and promotional items are chunked in clear blocks. Users can process a few items at a time, respecting STM limits.

Semantic Network Study

Semantic networks represent knowledge as a hierarchy of categories, properties, and examples, helping humans organize and retrieve information efficiently.
