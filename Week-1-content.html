Week 1 HCI Notes

Key Terms & Definitions

Term Definition
HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) Studies how humans interact with computers and systems, focusing on usability, accessibility, and efficiency.
Cognitive Science Explores the mind, including memory, perception, reasoning, and problem-solving.
Interaction Actions and communication between humans and computer systems.
STM (Short-Term Memory) Limited capacity memory; Miller’s Law suggests humans can hold 7 ± 2 items at once.
LTM (Long-Term Memory) Stores knowledge and experiences permanently, often organized in semantic networks.
Chunking Grouping information into meaningful units to reduce cognitive load and improve memorability.
Semantic Network A representation of knowledge where concepts are connected by categories, properties, and examples.

Interface & STM Examples

Examples of Interface Chunking & STM Observations
Example Interface / Observation Key Point
YouTube Side panel groups content into account/search, navigation (Home, Subscriptions, Explore), and personal items (History, Playlists). Reduces cognitive load by clear visual separation.
Canva Dashboard separates features into labeled sections for different task categories. Users can focus on one task category at a time.
DataCamp Interface organizes lessons and exercises into manageable sections. Supports STM; prevents cognitive overload.
Etsy Homepage content is grouped into labeled sections for products and categories. Users can process a limited number of items at once.
Semantic Network Knowledge represented hierarchically: Category → Sub-category → Example. Helps organize information in meaningful chunks.
